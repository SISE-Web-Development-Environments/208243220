1. 3 differences between web1, web2 and web3:
 1. Dynamic of the web:
In web1 the pages are only static.
In web2 pages are more dynamic, where a person can exchange information with others.
In web3 the pages are dynamic like web2 but can also to do refresh more often than web2

2. The purpose of the web:
In web1 the purpose is to connect information, with very little interaction between consumers. They can exchange information together but it was impossible to interact with the website.
In web2 the purpose was to connect people, the users can participate in the web with reading and writing and the web become bi-directional. Users of web2 has more interaction between theem with less control.
In web3 the purpose was to connect knowledge when the focus is on making data open and accessible. The Web contains a variety of data sets for the customers to use.

3. The way search engines works:
In web1 search engines retrieved macro contents. The search was fast but in most cases the results were inaccurate.
In web2 search engines retrieved tags with micro contents. The process of tagging was manual and covered only little amount of the web.
In web3 search engines will retrieve micro content texts which were tagged automatically. This will increase significant the amount of retrieval documents.
It will also can help to make more precise search. because tagging can solve part of the ambiguity that exist in the queries.
